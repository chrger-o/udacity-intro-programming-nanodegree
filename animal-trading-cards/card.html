Goat

Goats were one of the first animals to be domesticated -- around 9,000 years ago.

  • Scientific Name: Capra aegagrus hircus
  • Average Weight: 20 to 300 lbs
  • Average Lifespan: 8 to 12 years
  • Habitat: Farmlands worldwide

The domestic goat, a close relative of sheep, is appropriately named because it is the Greatest of All Time. Goats are regularly kept for their milk and their meat. On the internet, goats are best known for fainting and jumping (not in that order).
